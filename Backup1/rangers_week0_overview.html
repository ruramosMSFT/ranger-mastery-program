Rangers Program – Week 0 Overview

Selection, Baseline, Expectations

Week 0 happens 2–3 weeks before Week 1. Its job is to select the right cohort, capture baselines, and align expectations so the program can run smoothly and have impact.

1. Week 0 Goals

  • Select 6–8 Rangers against clear, behavior-based criteria.
  • Capture baselines: self-assessment and manager input.
  • Align expectations on time commitment, ways of working, and psychological safety.
  • Secure manager support so participants can fully engage in the program.

2. Selection

2.1 Eligibility criteria

Suggested criteria for candidates: - Shows signs of calm under pressure and does not escalate drama. - Demonstrates ownership for outcomes rather than habitual blame. - Communicates clearly with multiple stakeholders (support, engineering, product, account teams).

2.2 Manager nomination

Ask managers to nominate each candidate, capturing: - Why this person? - Top 2–3 strengths from the Gallup skills map. - Top 2–3 development areas.

2.3 Cohort composition

When choosing 6–8 participants, aim for: - A mix of profiles: some stronger in analysis/technical depth, others stronger in communication/relationships.

3. Baseline – Instruments and Data

3.1 The Self-assessment (participant)

Each selected participant completes the “Rangers Pre-Program Self-Assessment” (12 rating items + 3 open questions).

Send it with a short note: this is for growth, not performance rating.

Ask them to bring: - Their 1–2 highest-scored items (strengths). - Their 2–3 lowest-scored items (development priorities).

4. Week 0 Kickoff Session (60–90 minutes)

4.1 Objectives

  • Create a shared understanding of what a Ranger is and why this program exists.
  • Increase self-awareness using the self-assessment results.
  • Clarify logistics and prepare for Week 1.

4.2 Suggested agenda

Total time: 60–90 minutes (remote-friendly).

  1. Welcome and context (10–15 min)
    • Why Rangers exist: protect top customers during high-severity incidents.
    • Link to real crisis leadership: leading through disruption, not just “fixing tickets”.
  2. Program overview (10–15 min)
    • Duration, weekly time commitment, structure (pre-work + live sessions).
    • Emphasise cohort nature: this is a practice-based journey, not a webinar.
  3. Skills and mindset map (10–15 min)
    • Walk through the four pillars: perform under pressure, Extreme Ownership, executive communication, personal operating system.
    • Connect to the kind of crisis leader you want Rangers to be.
  4. Self-assessment reflection (20–25 min)
    • Plenary: Providae each individual space to elaborate on expectations
  5. Cohort working agreement (15–20 min)
    • Co-create simple norms, such as:
      • Attendance: live sessions are protected time.
      • Psychological safety: stories stay in the cohort; focus on learning, not blame.
      • Practice mindset: everyone will be “on stage”, mistakes in simulations are expected.
      • Feedback norms: specific, behavior-based feedback (what you saw, impact, alternative).
  6. Closing and next steps (5–10 min)
    • Recap key points.
    • Confirm Week 1 pre-work and schedule.
    • Ask each Ranger to write a one-line intention: “By the end of this program, I want to be the kind of Ranger who…”.

5. Week 0 Outputs

By the end of Week 0 you should have: - Final cohort list with a short rationale for each participant. - Completed self-assessments stored for comparison at Week 7. - Each Ranger’s initial focus areas (based on their lowest self-assessment items). - A one-page cohort working agreement summarising norms and expectations.

These give you a clean starting point for the full 8-week Rangers program.
