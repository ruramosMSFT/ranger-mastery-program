Ranger Mastery Program

Week 0 Overview

Week 0 happens 2 to 3 weeks before Week 1. Its job is to select the right cohort, capture baselines, and align expectations so the program can run smoothly and have impact.

Home Self-Assessment Program Model
Selection, Baseline, Expectations

Week 0 Goals

  • Select 4 to 6 Rangers against clear, behavior-based criteria.
  • Capture baselines through self-assessment and manager input.
  • Align expectations on time commitment, ways of working, and psychological safety.
  • Secure manager support so participants can fully engage in the program.
Selection

Eligibility Criteria

  • Shows signs of calm under pressure and does not escalate drama.
  • Demonstrates ownership for outcomes rather than habitual blame.
  • Communicates clearly with support, engineering, product, and account teams.
Manager Nomination

What Managers Provide

  • Why this person is being nominated.
  • Top 2 to 3 strengths from the Gallup skills map.
  • Top 2 to 3 development areas.
Cohort Composition

What to Balance

  • A mix of analysis, technical depth, communication, and relationship strengths.
  • Enough diversity for peer learning while keeping the group small enough for practice.
Baseline Data

Self-Assessment

  • Each selected participant completes the Rangers Pre-Program Self-Assessment.
  • The assessment is for growth, not performance rating.
  • Participants bring their top strengths and development priorities to kickoff.
Kickoff Session

Suggested Agenda: 60 to 90 Minutes

  1. Welcome and context: why Rangers exist and how the program connects to crisis leadership.
  2. Program overview: duration, weekly time commitment, structure, and cohort expectations.
  3. Skills and mindset map: pressure performance, Extreme Ownership, executive communication, and personal operating system.
  4. Self-assessment reflection: give each participant space to elaborate on expectations and focus areas.
  5. Cohort working agreement: attendance, psychological safety, practice mindset, and behavior-based feedback norms.
  6. Closing and next steps: confirm Week 1 pre-work and ask each Ranger to write a one-line intention.
Outputs

What Week 0 Should Produce

  • Final cohort list with a short rationale for each participant.
  • Completed self-assessments stored for comparison at Week 7.
  • Each Ranger's initial focus areas based on their lowest self-assessment items.
  • A one-page cohort working agreement summarising norms and expectations.

These outputs create a clean starting point for the full 8-week Rangers program.
