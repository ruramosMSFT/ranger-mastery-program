Ranger Mastery Program

High-Severity Leadership & Performance Program

A structured program model for developing calm, accountable, and clear crisis leaders in high-severity customer incidents.

Back to Program Home Download Original PDF

Program Snapshot

Audience: Senior support engineers / escalation owners who regularly handle high-severity incidents.

Cohort size: 4 to 6 participants per cohort.

Duration: 8 weeks (6 program weeks and one week for inverted shadowing), approximately 5 hours per person per week.

Format: Hybrid (remote-friendly), using self-paced content plus live discussions, simulations and debriefs.

Program Objectives

  • Build reliable performance under pressure for high-severity incidents.
  • Strengthen leadership behaviors based on Extreme Ownership (ownership, prioritization).
  • Upgrade executive communication and difficult-conversation skills.
  • Establish sustainable personal operating systems (time, boundaries, recovery).

Delivery Model

Self-paced learning
  • Short book excerpts, summaries and Linkedin trainings
  • The Emergency Mind (Dan Dworkis).
  • Extreme Ownership (Jocko Willink).
  • Crucial Conversations and HBR Having Difficult Conversations.
  • Perform Under Pressure.
  • Dialed In.
  • Peak Performance.
  • High-Stakes Leadership.
Live cohort sessions
  • 120 minutes weekly.
  • Mix of incident simulations, role plays, debriefs, and small-group breakouts.
  • Led by on-the-job leaders (senior ICs / special guests) for realism.
On-the-job application
  • Participants apply tools during real incidents each week, such as using the executive update template, running debriefs, and experimenting with boundaries.
  • Capture short micro-cases for discussion.
Peer partner system
  • Each participant is paired with a program leader.

Curriculum Structure (8 Weeks)

Week 0 - Selection, Baseline, Expectations
  • Purpose: align stakeholders, select participants intentionally, establish baselines and inputs.
  • Manager nominations based on observed behaviors: ownership, composure potential, and communication.
  • Simple self-assessment on key skills: communication, decision-making, stress management, and leadership.
  • Short questionnaire aligned with desired psychological profile: emotional stability, adaptability, internal locus of control, service orientation, and comfort with ambiguity.
